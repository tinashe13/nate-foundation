About Us



Background

The Sanyanga family started the NATE Foundation after two of their three children were diagnosed with Fanconi Anemia (FA), a rare genetic disease. The Foundation seeks to establish a center to assist other people in the different phases of treatment upon diagnosis of the condition. The Foundation has an oversight and implementation unit to assist with the day to day work of the organisation. The work of the Foundation is underpinned by the founders’ desire to see that many other children and patients with FA get the adequate help that they need in order for them to live healthy lives to the fullest. Giving hope to many disadvantaged children and families that are experiencing challenges in accessing treatment for the condition is at the core of Foundation’s work.



The Mission

To provide information, counseling and medical support for FA patients and families.



The Vision

Centre for research, counseling and medical support for FA in Africa and beyond.



Strategic Objectives

The main objectives of the Foundation are:

  • • provision of support to families and patients with FA including medical research, and training for specialized medical practitioners and care givers
  • • provision of psychosocial support to families and caregivers of patients with FA, including provision of comfort space and support
  • • provision of a resource centre for research, publications equipped communication technologies capability

Foundation Units


Medical Unit (MU)


The MU will focus on the special medical needs associated with FA including establishing a bone marrow transplant unit, which will assist in conducting affordable transplant, including training of medical personnel on associated procedures.



Knowledge and Resource Unit (KRU)

There is limited knowledge in communities on FA, including the medical fraternity. The Foundation will establish a knowledge and resource centre with comfort rooms for families.



Counselling Unit (CU)

The emotional and psychosocial toll for care givers and families affected by FA requires skilled counselling. The Foundation will, research on existing counseling services and gaps, develop a curriculum to train counselors, establish and resource a counseling training centre.
